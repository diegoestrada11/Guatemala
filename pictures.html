Antigua Guatemala, commonly referred to as Antigua, is a captivating city nestled in the central highlands of Guatemala. Steeped in rich history, this UNESCO World Heritage site exudes charm and cultural significance. Antigua was once the capital of Guatemala until a series of devastating earthquakes led to its relocation to present-day Guatemala City. Today, Antigua stands as a testament to its colonial past, with its well-preserved Spanish Baroque-influenced architecture, cobbled streets, and colorful facades. The city is renowned for its beautiful churches, such as the iconic La Merced and the majestic Cathedral of Santiago, which showcase exquisite architectural details and religious artifacts. Antigua is also famous for its vibrant celebrations, particularly during Semana Santa (Holy Week), when elaborate processions fill the streets with fervor and devotion. Visitors to Antigua can explore its captivating plazas, browse through bustling markets brimming with local crafts and textiles, and savor the flavors of traditional Guatemalan cuisine. With its enchanting ambiance, historical significance, and natural beauty, Antigua Guatemala continues to captivate the hearts of both locals and travelers alike.
